About Me

Hi! I’m Jayamine Alupotha, a cryptographer and computer scientist passionate about building secure, privacy-preserving systems for the post-quantum era. I’m currently a Postdoctoral Researcher at the University of Bern, where I work on cutting-edge problems in cryptography, blockchain technology, and distributed systems.

My academic journey took me through a Ph.D. at Queensland University of Technology, where I explored zero-knowledge contracts, confidential transactions, and zero-history blockchains. Today, I continue this path by publishing research, building practical cryptographic tools, and contributing to the open-source community.

Some of my work includes:

  • LACT+ – a post-quantum confidential transaction protocol
  • Rahas – a zero-knowledge programming language
  • TxHelper – a blockchain transaction simulator

Beyond research, I love teaching, mentoring, and sharing ideas that push the boundaries of secure computation. I’m currently seeking opportunities as a Lecturer in Cybersecurity or Cryptography, where I can combine my research expertise and teaching experience to inspire and equip the next generation of security professionals.

If you're a fellow researcher, hiring academic, or someone curious about cryptography, feel free to connect or explore my work!

Cryptographic Implementations

  • LACT+ – A lattice-based, post-quantum confidential transaction protocol designed for secure and private payments in decentralized systems. It enables transaction aggregation and scalability, making it a strong candidate for future quantum-safe cryptocurrencies.
  • Rahas – A zero-knowledge programming language built for privacy-focused applications. Based on the Confidential Integer Processing (CIP) model, Rahas allows developers to express complex ZK proofs with a streamlined syntax tailored for security and performance.
  • TxHelper – A simulation tool for blockchain developers that compares twelve distinct transaction models. TxHelper helps researchers and developers test the performance, privacy, and suitability of transaction structures in various decentralized app contexts.
